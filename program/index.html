Day 1 — July 7th: Evolution

Theme Leads: Finlay Maguire & Caroline Ritter

Phylogenetic, phylodynamic, landscape, and lateral gene-transfer analyses can be integrated to characterize pathogen evolution and transmission across interconnected human, animal, and environmental systems. This computational work underpins One Health approaches by generating actionable evidence for risk assessment and preparedness. Findings directly inform policy decisions and multi-sector systems change, enabling more resilient surveillance architectures, targeted interventions, and cross-disciplinary governance.

Day 2 — July 8th: Environmental Ecology and Microbial Communities

Theme Leads: Rob Beiko & Natalie Diether

Field-based environmental sequencing, eDNA, community ecology, and microbiome research to characterize how ecological interactions shape health across human, animal, and environmental systems. These insights strengthen One Health approaches by guiding microbiome stewardship, informing environmental and public-health policy, and enabling systems-level changes that improve ecosystem resilience, surveillance, and resource management.

Day 3 — July 9th: Applied One Health Research

Theme Leads: Zhenyu Cheng & Tara Penney

Applied laboratory and translational research on novel therapeutics, vaccines, and outbreak investigation aimed at mitigating risks across interconnected human, animal, and environmental domains. This One Health–aligned work generates evidence for policy and operational decision-making and incorporates intentional trust-building and policy engagement strategies to enhance impact. By supporting transparent communication, cross-sector collaboration, and the integration of scientific findings into practice, it advances systems-level change in surveillance, governance, and emergency preparedness.
